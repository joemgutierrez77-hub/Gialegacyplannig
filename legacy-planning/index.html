The Method

How infinite banking works

Five moves, in order. The policy is the vehicle; the discipline of borrowing against it — instead of from a bank — is the strategy.

Fund the policy

A dividend-paying whole life policy from a mutual carrier, designed with a paid-up additions rider so early cash value builds fast.

Let it compound

Cash value grows on a guaranteed schedule plus dividends — uninterrupted, tax-deferred, and shielded in many states from creditors.

Borrow against it

Need capital? Take a policy loan from the carrier with your cash value as collateral. Your full balance keeps compounding while you use their money.

Repay on your terms

No bank schedule, no credit check. Repay to restore capacity — or let the balance simply net against the death benefit.

Transfer the legacy

The death benefit passes to your beneficiaries — generally free of income tax — turning one policy into generational wealth.

Paid-Up Additions

Small, fully paid-up slices of extra insurance bought with rider dollars. They juice early cash value and compound dividends on dividends — the engine of an IBC design.

Policy loan

A loan from the carrier's general account secured by your cash value. It isn't a withdrawal — your cash value never leaves the policy, so compounding never stops.

Break-even point

The policy year when cash value first exceeds every premium dollar paid in. After it, the policy is worth more than it ever cost — permanently.

Loan capacity

How much you can borrow — typically 90–95% of cash value. It grows every year, whether or not you use it, and resets as loans are repaid.
